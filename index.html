This investigation exposes how Russian involvement in the Belarusian Nuclear Power Plant project appears to be a covert financial-political form of colonialism. The Astravets Nuclear Power Plant represents a unique case of nuclear colonialism where the infrastructure of colonial dependence arises from the conditions of overproduction of resources rather than their extraction. A small and almost non-material tablet of uranium becomes a basis of hughe colonial infrastructure that makes Belarus dependent on Russia both as a resource supplier and as a necessary consumer for the Astravyets NPP overproduced energy.

It is not a secret that the nuclear power plant is Alexander Lukashenko’s personal project. However, his hopes that the NPP will ensure Belarusian energy independence turned out to be nonsensical. The Astravyets NPP project leads to the infrastructural integration of the biggest producing Belarusian enterprises and civil sector into Russia and results in the dismantling of the Belarusian sovereignty. While Lukashenko continues to claim that “There is no greater value than a sovereign and independent Belarus,” he single-handedly surrenders the entire Belarusian energy sector to Russia through the failed nuclear project.
